Three Events That Lead Me To LaunchCode

High School Robot

My junior year of HighSchool, one of my classmates brought to school an actual tiny robot he made from the ground up himself. Being so young, I thought it was very interesting he could do this all by himself. Of course I asked him about a million questions, which all lead back to coding. This was the first time I had an interest in technology - but it was years before I gave it anymore thought because I doubted that I could ever be smart enough to work in tech.

Working at Invisalink

In 2019, I landed a job at an internet company called Invisalink. At first, I was only the receptionist. Eventually I was trained to the tech side of things, like working in routers from my browser. I definitely learned a lot from this company, and having said position sky-rockted my interest with having a career in tech, I just didn't know where to start.

Moving To Illinois

Fast forward to 2023, my family and I relocated to Alton, Illinois for work. My partner's aunt asked me one day what my career-interests were and I told her I would love to work in tech, but was restricted on going to school being that I am a stay at home mom. She told me about this program called "Coder-Girl" that a friend of hers had completed, and the rest is history! I researched LaunchCode, applied, and was accepted! I've fallen in love with tech and cannot wait to see where this program takes me.
